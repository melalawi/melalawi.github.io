Description

I've implemented a very simple example of quadtree cell decomposition in two-dimensional space.

The grid above represents the search space. Click anywhere in the grid to build obstacles and walls that will be considered when decomposing the space into a quadtree.

Generating rectangles: Every two clicks will define the corners of a rectangle.

Generating triangles: Hold down shift before the second click to define a triangle.

Clicking 'Begin Decomposition' will begin the cell decomposition process. A visual representation of the final tree will appear after the process is complete

Green blocks represent 'free' cells, while the red ones represent impassable cells.

Due to a hard limit imposed on the lowest possible granularity level for optimization reasons, the final tree may not perfectly align with the obstacles defined (especially evident with triangles).
